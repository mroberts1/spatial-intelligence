# Spatial Intelligence — Foundry Evaluation

Stable questions that should be answerable from this wiki. Re-run on every `/foundry-eval`. Thin/missing findings feed back into `wiki/_meta/index.md` as Open Questions and Candidates.

See `wiki/_meta/eval-usage.md` for how this document is actually used.

**Ratings:**
- `strong` — wiki has the concepts and they cite ≥2 sources (ideally mixed tiers)
- `thin` — wiki has the concepts but sourcing is weak or single-source
- `missing` — wiki can't answer

Questions are grouped by what they probe. Don't reorder or renumber — that breaks comparability across runs. Add new questions at the bottom.

---

## Definitional / framing (does the wiki know what it's about?)

**Q1. What is spatial intelligence as a construct — and how do the psychometric tradition (Thurstone, Gardner), the cognitive-neuroscience tradition (O'Keefe, Moser), and the computational tradition (robotics, vision-language models) each define it, and where do those definitions genuinely conflict?**
Probes: domain scope, ability to hold competing definitions without collapsing them. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q2. What distinguishes embodied spatial cognition (Gibson's affordances, Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality, enactive approaches) from abstract geometric reasoning — and what empirical evidence bears on which account is more adequate?**
Probes: the vault's productive fault line; should be tractable once 3-4 foundational sources are in. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q3. What is a cognitive map — Tolman's original usage, its reinterpretation in the hippocampal-place-cell literature, and subsequent critiques (does the rat really have a "map" in any rich sense, or is the term doing too much work)?**
Probes: anchor-concept fidelity, ability to track semantic drift in a foundational term. Expected early rating: missing.

## Neural substrates (does the wiki go deep on the neuroscience?)

**Q4. How do place cells, grid cells, and head-direction cells divide spatial encoding labour — and what does their dissociation (e.g. grid cells survive hippocampal lesions; place cells don't survive entorhinal lesions) reveal about the architecture of spatial memory?**
Probes: technical depth on hippocampal-entorhinal circuit. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q5. What does the 2014 Nobel Prize work (O'Keefe, Moser & Moser) consolidate, and what open questions did it leave — particularly around remapping, rate coding vs. population coding, and whether the grid-cell coordinate system is truly Euclidean?**
Probes: ability to hold scientific consensus alongside unresolved questions. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q6. How do spatial deficits in hippocampal amnesia (H.M., R.B., patient E.P.) constrain theories of spatial cognition — and what is the relationship between spatial memory and episodic/semantic memory more broadly?**
Probes: clinical/neuropsychological side of spatial cognition. Expected early rating: missing.

## Developmental and cross-species (does the wiki hold comparative depth?)

**Q7. What can cross-species comparisons — Tinbergen's digger wasps, Wehner's desert ants, Collett's bees, Emlen's indigo buntings, Epstein's London taxi drivers — tell us about the evolutionary origins of spatial intelligence, and which spatial capacities appear to be phylogenetically ancient vs. uniquely mammalian?**
Probes: comparative and evolutionary framing. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q8. How does spatial cognition develop in human infants and children — landmark use, allocentric vs. egocentric frames, the development of survey knowledge — and what does this developmental trajectory reveal about the underlying cognitive architecture?**
Probes: developmental side of the domain. Expected early rating: missing.

## Designed space and phenomenology (does the wiki hold the non-cognitive-science perspectives?)

**Q9. What is space syntax, how does Hillier & Hanson's configurational analysis work, and what has it revealed about the relationship between spatial configuration and human movement/occupancy that simpler environmental models miss?**
Probes: architectural/urban-design thread. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q10. How does Kevin Lynch's concept of imageability and the five elements (paths, edges, districts, nodes, landmarks) hold up against subsequent cognitive-mapping research — and what did it get right, what did it miss, and what has replaced or extended it?**
Probes: the urban-design / cognitive-science intersection. Expected early rating: missing.

## Computational / AI (does the wiki track the moving frontier?)

**Q11. Where do current large vision-language models (GPT-4V, Gemini, Claude) actually fail at spatial reasoning tasks — systematic vs. incidental failures — and what do those failure modes reveal about what the models are and aren't doing?**
Probes: the computational thread, fast-moving. Expected early rating: missing.

**Q12. What is the state of embodied AI and spatial reasoning in robotics — SLAM, scene graphs, neural radiance fields, foundation models for manipulation — and how does the robotics framing of spatial intelligence relate to the cognitive-science framing?**
Probes: robotics/embodied-AI thread. Expected early rating: missing.
