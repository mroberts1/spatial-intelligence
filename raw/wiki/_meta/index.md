## Research Threads

- **Embodied vs. abstract spatial cognition.** The central fault line: is spatial intelligence grounded in bodily experience (Merleau-Ponty, Gibson) or decomposable into abstract geometric representations? Needs primary cognitive science and computational sources.
- **Neural substrates of space.** Place cells (O'Keefe), grid cells (Moser), head-direction cells — the hippocampal/entorhinal system as a spatial computing substrate. Well-studied; needs classic and contemporary sources.
- **Computational spatial reasoning.** How transformer-based and other AI architectures handle spatial tasks — vision-language models, embodied AI, robotics. Active debate about whether current models constitute genuine spatial intelligence.
- **Designed space and perception.** Space syntax (Hillier & Hanson), architectural phenomenology, wayfinding, urban legibility (Lynch). The designed-environment side of the domain, less covered in cognitive-science framing.
- **Cross-species spatial cognition.** Tolman's cognitive map experiments, homing pigeons, desert ants, bees — spatial intelligence in non-human animals as a comparative baseline.

## Open Questions

- What is spatial intelligence, exactly — and which definition (psychometric, cognitive, computational, phenomenological) is most productive for a cross-disciplinary vault?
- How do place cells and grid cells divide spatial encoding labour, and what does their dissociation reveal about the architecture of spatial memory?
- What distinguishes embodied spatial cognition (Gibson's affordances, Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality) from abstract geometric reasoning — and where do current AI models fall on that spectrum?
- How does Kevin Lynch's concept of urban imageability relate to cognitive map formation — and what has been added since *The Image of the City* (1960)?
- What can cross-species comparisons (insects, birds, mammals) tell us about the evolutionary origins of spatial intelligence that human-only studies cannot?
- Where do current large vision-language models (GPT-4V, Gemini, Claude) actually fail at spatial reasoning, and what do those failure modes reveal?
- How has the field's definition of spatial intelligence shifted between Thurstone (1938), Gardner (multiple intelligences, 1983), and contemporary neuroscience/robotics framings?
