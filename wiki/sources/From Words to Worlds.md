## Summary

Fei-Fei Li (cofounder of World Labs, original architect of ImageNet) argues that spatial intelligence—the ability to perceive, reason about, and interact with physical and virtual worlds—is AI's next frontier. While LLMs excel at abstract, text-based reasoning, they remain "wordsmiths in the dark" without grounding in spatial understanding. World models with three core capabilities (generative, multimodal, interactive) will unlock spatial intelligence and transform creativity, robotics, scientific discovery, and human flourishing.

## Key Concepts

### Spatial Intelligence as Core Human Capability
- Foundational to human cognition, evolution, and survival
- Drives creativity, imagination, and practical problem-solving
- Scaffolding for abstract reasoning (e.g., Eratosthenes' geometric proof, DNA structure discovery)
- Essential for embodied tasks: navigation, manipulation, coordination

### The Limits of LLMs
- Current AI excels at abstract knowledge (text) but lacks spatial grounding
- Struggle with distance estimation, object rotation, maze navigation, physics prediction
- Generated videos lose coherence after seconds
- Cannot effectively drive robots, guide autonomous systems, or enable immersive experiences

### Three Pillars of World Models

1. **Generative**: Generate worlds with perceptual, geometric, and physical consistency
   - Must follow semantic/physical instructions while maintaining coherence
   - Explicit, observable state representation (not just latent)
   - History-aware: past states inform present understanding

2. **Multimodal**: Process diverse inputs (images, video, text, gestures, actions)
   - Output complete world states from partial information
   - Blend visual fidelity with semantic understanding

3. **Interactive**: Predict next world states based on input actions
   - Given action + goal, produce consistent next state
   - Advanced models can predict actions given goals

### Applications

**Creativity**: Filmmakers, game designers, architects using world models (Marble) to rapidly create explorable 3D worlds without traditional overhead

**Robotics**: Training data via simulation, collaborative human-robot systems, diverse embodied forms (humanoid, soft robots, nanobots)

**Science & Healthcare**: Molecular simulation, drug discovery, diagnostics, ambient patient monitoring

**Education**: Immersive learning, tangible abstractions, safe practice environments (surgery, engineering)

## Technical Challenges

- Defining a universal task function as elegant as next-token prediction
- Processing 2D image/video signals to extract 3D spatial information at scale
- New architectures beyond current MLLMs and diffusion models (3D/4D-aware tokenization, spatial memory)
- Synthetic data, sensor systems, neural simulation methods
